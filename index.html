About

I graduated from Worcester Polytechnic Institute in 2017 with a BS in Mathematics with a minor in Computer Science. I originally went to school for Math and Computer Science because I love to solve problems. After working in quality assurance at a software company, I realized I wanted to work in healthcare to help others. I enjoy working on a team, helping people, teaching people, and solving problems, so nursing felt like a great fit. I graduated in Jan 2021 with a BS in Nursing. I have been a nurse for over 2 years at the University of Utah, and I miss using my analytical and technical skills. In August of 2022, I began to refresh my computer science knowledge. During my first undergraduate degree, I had exposure to C++, MatLab, R, and Python. I took courses such as, Data Structures, Foundations of Computer Science, Graph Theory, Object-Oriented Design Concepts, and Techniques of Programming. I have been building upon this knowledge by teaching myself JavaScript, Python, HTML, CSS, and SQL, as well as reviewing data structures, algorithms, and object oriented programming. I have been enjoying learning new skills, and I continue to learn each day. I have discovered that many skills from nursing transfer well to software engineering, such as my attention to detail, empathy, problem-solving skills, and ability to work in high-pressure situations. I am looking to transition from nursing to software engineering.
